PinMar Organs Opus I

Construction Photos

These are pictures from the Rodgers 925 Hauptwerk conversion. The entire console was gutted and built from the ground up, only using the existing keyboards, pedalboard, and pistons. New Syndyne solenoid drawknobs and rocker tabs were purchased, along with appropriate driver boards. LCD status screens were programmed and installed in the leftover space originally taken by the extra Rodgers couplers.

The console is connected to the computer in an adjoining room via an Ethernet cable. A 14 channel speaker system was designed and built to suit the space where the organ was to be installed. Several 12V trigger lines run from the console to the speakers to facilitate a "one button" power system. The total time from when the power button is pressed until the organ is ready to play is about 5 seconds, quite similar to a traditional pipe organ.

This organ is installed in Mary Our Queen Catholic Church in Norcross, GA.
